Scalar Multiplication of a Matrix

When a matrix \(A\) is multiplied by a scalar \(k\), the operation is defined as multiplying each element of \(A\) by \(k\). Given a matrix \(A\): \[ A = \begin{pmatrix} a_{11} & a_{12} \\ a_{21} & a_{22} \end{pmatrix} \] And a scalar \(k\), the result of the scalar multiplication \(kA\) is: \[ kA = \begin{pmatrix} ka_{11} & ka_{12} \\ ka_{21} & ka_{22} \end{pmatrix} \] This operation scales the matrix by \(k\) without changing its dimension or the relative proportion of its elements.
