Adjusted R-Squared (\( R^2_{\text{adj}} \))

Adjusted R-squared (\( R^2_{\text{adj}} \)) is an improved version of R-squared that accounts for the number of predictors in a regression model. It prevents overfitting by adjusting for the number of independent variables.

Mathematical Formulation

The formula for Adjusted R-squared is:

\( R^2_{\text{adj}} = 1 - \left( \frac{(1 - R^2) \times (n - 1)}{n - p - 1} \right) \)

  • \( R^2 \): The regular R-squared value.
  • \( n \): The number of observations (data points).
  • \( p \): The number of independent variables (predictors).

Implementation Steps

  1. Calculate the regular R-squared value.
  2. Determine the number of observations (\( n \)) and predictors (\( p \)).
  3. Apply the formula to get \( R^2_{\text{adj}} \).

Example Calculation

Given:

  • \( R^2 = 0.85 \)
  • \( n = 10 \) (total observations)
  • \( p = 2 \) (number of predictors)

Step-by-step:

\( R^2_{\text{adj}} = 1 - \left( \frac{(1 - 0.85) \times (10 - 1)}{10 - 2 - 1} \right) \)

Final value:

\( R^2_{\text{adj}} = 0.80179 \)
