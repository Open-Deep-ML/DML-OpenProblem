Understanding Normal Distribution

The normal distribution, also known as the Gaussian distribution, is a continuous probability distribution widely used in statistics. It describes data that clusters around a mean or average, with fewer values farther from the mean.

Mathematical Formulation

The probability density function (PDF) of a normal distribution with mean \( \mu \) and standard deviation \( \sigma \) for a given value \( x \) is given by: \[ f(x) = \frac{1}{\sigma \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2}\right) \]

Implementation Steps

1. **Define Parameters**: Set the mean \( \mu \) and standard deviation \( \sigma \) of the distribution. 2. **Apply PDF Formula**: For a specified \( x \), plug in the values of \( \mu \), \( \sigma \), and \( x \) into the PDF formula. 3. **Calculate Probability Density**: Compute the result, which gives the probability density of \( x \).

Example Calculation

Given: - **Mean** \( \mu \) = 0 - **Standard Deviation** \( \sigma \) = 1 - **Value** \( x \) = 1 The probability density at \( x = 1 \) is calculated as: \[ f(1) = \frac{1}{1 \times \sqrt{2 \pi}} \exp\left(-\frac{(1 - 0)^2}{2 \times 1^2}\right) \approx 0.24197 \]

Applications

The normal distribution is essential in various fields, including: - **Statistics**: Many statistical tests assume normal distribution. - **Machine Learning**: Assists in modeling and understanding data distributions. - **Natural and Social Sciences**: Often used to model measurement errors, natural events, and human characteristics.
